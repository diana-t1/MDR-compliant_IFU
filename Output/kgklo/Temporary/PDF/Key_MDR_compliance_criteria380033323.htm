Key MDR compliance criteria

MDR specifies requirements for information supplied with the device in Annex I, Chapter III, outlining the essential details that must appear on the device itself, its packaging, and within the IFU.

Clear and Understandable Language

IFU should be written in simple, understandable language, considering the user’s technical knowledge, experience, and training. Where necessary, visual aids such as diagrams and illustrations should be included to improve comprehension.

 Defining Device Purpose and Safety Considerations

Every IFU should define the device’s purpose, outlining who should use it, when it should be used, and any indications or contraindications to be aware of. Safety is paramount, so IFU must also highlight potential risks, side effects, and warnings, allowing users to take the right precautions. MDR also requires performance specifications, ensuring that professionals know exactly what to expect from the device and how to verify its suitability.

 Special Considerations for Implantable and Reusable Devices

For implantable or reusable devices, IFU must go even further, detailing the materials used, how to clean, disinfect, and reprocess them, and when they should no longer be reused. Devices that need assembly, sterilization, or calibration must have step-by-step guidance to prevent errors. If a device emits radiation, IFU must provide protective measures to minimize exposure risks.

 Communicating Risks and Using Standardized Symbols

IFU must clearly communicate residual risks, listing warnings, contraindications, and precautions to ensure user safety. Where appropriate, internationally recognized symbols should be used in place of long descriptions, provided they conform to harmonized standards.

 Ensuring Accessibility and Regular Updates

IFU must be kept up to date and readily accessible, including on the manufacturer’s website, to ensure users always have access to the latest safety and performance information. These requirements emphasize user-friendly design, transparency, and safety, ensuring that medical devices can be used effectively and without unnecessary risk.

 Serious Incidents Reporting Requirements

Every IFU must inform users that serious incidents related to the device must be reported to both the manufacturer and relevant authorities.
