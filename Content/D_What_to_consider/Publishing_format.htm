Publishing format

The choice of publishing format plays a crucial role in ensuring compliance and usability across different markets.

Traditional print format

Printed IFU remain a widely used format, especially for single-use devices and products intended for lay users. Paper-based instructions are easy to reference and do not require additional technology to access. However, MDR mandates that printed IFU must be legible, well-structured, and available in the official languages of the countries where the device is marketed. Manufacturers must also ensure that printed IFU are updated regularly, which can be costly and time-consuming..

 Electronic IFU (e-IFU)

With the rise of digital healthcare, electronic IFU (e-IFU) are becoming more common, offering searchability, accessibility, and real-time updates. MDR allows e-IFU under Regulation (EU) No 207/2012, particularly for professional-use devices. Digital formats can include PDFs, web-based IFU, or interactive applications, making it easier for users to find specific information quickly. e-IFU also support screen readers and accessibility tools, improving usability for visually impaired users.

 Hybrid formats

Some manufacturers opt for a hybrid approach, combining printed IFU with digital access. This can include QR codes or RFID tags on packaging that link to an online IFU repository. This method ensures compliance with MDR while reducing paper waste and allowing users to access the latest version of the IFU.

 Multimedia and interactive IFU

For complex medical devices, interactive IFU – such as video tutorials, animations, or augmented reality guides – can enhance user understanding. While MDR does not explicitly mandate multimedia formats, they can be valuable for training healthcare professionals and improving patient safety.
