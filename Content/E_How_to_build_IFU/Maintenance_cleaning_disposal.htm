Maintenance, cleaning, and disposal guidelines

Proper maintenance, cleaning, and disposal are essential for ensuring device longevity, safety, and regulatory compliance under Medical Device Regulation (MDR 2017/745). IFU must provide clear, step-by-step instructions tailored to the device type, whether it's reusable, sterilizable, or single-use.

Maintenance: Outline routine checks, calibration steps, and preventive measures to keep the device functioning correctly. Specify any consumable parts that require replacement.

Cleaning: Provide detailed cleaning procedures, including approved disinfectants, sterilization techniques, and handling precautions. Visual guides can improve clarity.

Disposal: Include safe disposal instructions, especially for devices containing hazardous materials. Highlight environmental considerations and applicable local regulations for medical waste disposal.

Often these instruction must reflect the properties of the device which were proven by technical testing or conforming to standards such as:

Standard or regulation Description
ISO 17664:2017 Specifies requirements for processing reusable medical devices, including cleaning, disinfection, and sterilization
EN 62366-1 Covers usability engineering, ensuring IFU are designed for safe and effective device handling
ISO 10993-1 Addresses biocompatibility, ensuring cleaning and sterilization methods do not compromise patient safety
IEC 60878 Provides standardized symbols for medical device labeling, including those related to maintenance and disposal
EU waste regulations Determines waste disposal, including medical device disposal
EN 60529 Defines Ingress Protection (IP) ratings, which indicate how well a device is protected against solid particles and water.
