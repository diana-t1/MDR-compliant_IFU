Clear warnings and precautions

Warnings and precautions in IFU must be crystal clear because they directly impact user safety and regulatory compliance. If IFU contain vague, complex, or poorly worded warnings, users—whether healthcare professionals or patients—might misinterpret crucial safety instructions, leading to device misuse, health risks, or regulatory violations.

Use formatting to Your advantage:

1. Use bold & highlighted text for critical warnings

  • Bold key warnings to make them stand out.

  • Use color-coded sections (e.g., red for danger, yellow for caution) where applicable.

  • Consider boxed warnings for high-risk information.

 2. Organize warnings in a clear hierarchy

  • Start with general safety precautions, followed by device-specific risks.

  • Use bullet points or numbered lists to break down complex warnings.

  • Ensure logical flow, grouping related precautions together.

 3. Incorporate standardized symbols & icons

  • Use internationally recognized safety symbols to enhance clarity.

  • Include visual indicators for risks (e.g., radiation, electrical hazards).

  • Ensure symbols conform to harmonized standards for consistency.

 4. Ensure readability & accessibility

  • Use high-contrast fonts for easy reading.

  • Provide large-print versions for visually impaired users.

  • Ensure electronic IFU (e-IFU) support screen readers and text-to-speech tools.
